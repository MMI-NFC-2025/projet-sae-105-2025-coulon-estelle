Interstellar

Cinema as a Rehabilitation Tool

A film by Christopher Nolan, blending science fiction and drama, where humanity seeks a new hope among the stars. For 169 minutes, Nolan takes us on an emotional journey through black holes, the relativity of time, and the unbreakable bond between a father and his daughter.

Learn More About the Matilda Effect

Synopsis

In the near future, Earth has become inhospitable. Dust storms ravage crops, and humanity faces extinction. Cooper, a former NASA pilot turned farmer, is recruited for a desperate mission: to traverse a mysteriously appeared wormhole near Saturn to explore potentially habitable planets in another galaxy.

Accompanied by a team of scientists, Cooper embarks on a journey beyond the stars, where one hour can equate to several years on Earth. The film explores themes of love, sacrifice, and the survival of the human species while remaining true to the laws of physics.

The Women in This Film

In Interstellar, Dr. Amelia Brand (Anne Hathaway) embodies a brilliant and determined scientist, whose theories, initially ignored, prove crucial for the future of humanity.

Her journey illustrates the Matilda effect, a phenomenon where the contributions of women scientists are often underestimated or overlooked.

Through her, the film pays tribute to these forgotten researchers while exploring real notions of astrophysics, black holes, relativity, and time distortions echoing major discoveries about pulsars.

The Science Behind Interstellar

Kip Thorne, theoretical physicist and Nobel Prize winner in Physics 2017, played a crucial role in the creation of Interstellar.

He developed the equations that allowed for the accurate visualization of Gargantua, the supermassive black hole in the film. This visualization was so precise that it led to the publication of two scientific papers.

The rendering of Gargantua realistically shows how an accretion disk would appear around a rotating black hole. Gravitational lensing effects distort spacetime and make visible the part of the disk located behind the black hole.

Christopher Nolan insisted that the science be respected as much as possible, making Interstellar a science fiction film very close to current scientific models. This approach also helps explain complex concepts to the general public.
