Glossary

List of terms to explain or deepen

Find here all the scientific terms and important concepts to better understand the universe, pulsars, and the discoveries of Jocelyn Bell Burnell.

Discover Her Story

Pulsar

A rapidly rotating neutron star that emits beams of electromagnetic radiation. Pulsars are detectable from Earth as regular pulses.

Neutron Star

An extremely dense remnant of a massive star that has exploded in a supernova. A neutron star typically has the mass of the Sun compressed into a sphere about 20 km in diameter.

Supernova

The explosion of a star at the end of its life. Supernovae are among the most energetic events in the universe and can shine as brightly as an entire galaxy.

General Relativity

The theory of gravitation formulated by Albert Einstein in 1915. It describes gravity as a distortion of space-time caused by the presence of mass and energy.

Time Dilation

A phenomenon predicted by relativity where time passes more slowly in a strong gravitational field or at speeds close to that of light.

Wormhole

A hypothetical passage through space-time that could create shortcuts between two distant points in the universe. Also called an Einstein-Rosen bridge.

Accretion Disk

A structure formed by matter orbiting around a massive object like a black hole. Friction in the disk heats the matter, which then emits intense radiation.

Gravitational Lensing

A phenomenon where light is bent as it passes near a massive object, creating distorted or multiple images of distant objects.

Electromagnetic Radiation

A form of energy that travels through space in the form of waves. Includes radio waves, microwaves, infrared, visible light, ultraviolet, X-rays, and gamma rays.

Matilda Effect

A systematic bias of minimizing or denying the contributions of women scientists to research. Named after Matilda Joslyn Gage, who documented this phenomenon.

Radio Astronomy

A branch of astronomy that studies celestial objects through the radio waves they emit. A technique used by Jocelyn Bell to discover pulsars.
